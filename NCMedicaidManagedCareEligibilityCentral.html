Eligibility Spans

One row per coverage-group span — the state's answer to “from when to when, and under which door did this person enter?” Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishA span is a promise with dates on it — and North Carolina’s spans include a promise no other state’s data makes: the conversion. On December 1, 2023, hundreds of thousands of FP01 spans (the Be Smart limited family-planning benefit) closed with reason “converted” and full AEG spans opened at the same midnight — a benefit upgrade wall with no application anywhere in it. Register F rides that ladder all the way up: limited → full → dual, three groups in three years.

The Transformation

One row per delivery span: a standard plan (all five launched July 1, 2021), a Tailored Plan (July 1, 2024, LME/MCO lineage), or Medicaid Direct — the unmanaged lane. Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishNorth Carolina ran fee-for-service longer than almost anyone, then transformed all at once: July 1, 2021, five standard plans, 1.6 million people, one day — register D’s members carry that wall inside their own delivery histories, FFS through June, plan from July. The populations judged too complex for the big bang — serious mental illness, I/DD, TBI — waited three more years for Tailored Plans: regional entities descended from the county-agency LME/MCO system, a public lineage folded into full-benefit plans (where Pennsylvania carved its county side out beside the plans, NC absorbed its county lineage into them). And Medicaid Direct is the transformation’s negative space: exempt and excluded populations, including tribal members the law lets stay unmanaged. Three delivery worlds, all created in the last five years.

What the File Says

North Carolina really does speak X12N 834 — the state’s roster files to its standard and Tailored Plans. Pick a member and read the transaction, segment by segment, with a plain-language note on every line.

In plain EnglishAn 834 is the state telling a health plan: this person is yours now — and North Carolina’s files carry the youngest history in the series. December 2023’s files were the largest single-day additions any expansion ever generated, most of them conversions from a limited benefit. Register E’s members generate encounters with service codes for food boxes and security deposits — the Healthy Opportunities fee schedule. Medicaid Direct members generate no plan 834 at all; theirs is the state’s own record, shown in the same shape for teaching.
