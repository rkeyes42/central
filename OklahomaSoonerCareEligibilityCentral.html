Eligibility Spans

One row per coverage-group span — the state's answer to “from when to when, and under which door did this person enter?” Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishA span is a promise with dates on it — and in Oklahoma the promise can be made the same day it’s asked for: the online application runs real-time eligibility, one of the first in the country to say yes before the applicant logs off. Spans close for reasons — renewals that didn't come back, Medicare arriving — and one reason appears here that Mississippi’s file, three states east, cannot produce: category change into the expansion group. Register D is built on it. Watch also for SP07 spans: SoonerPlan is a real eligibility value that is not full coverage — family planning only. Read the benefit, not just the yes.

SoonerSelect

One row per delivery span: which SoonerSelect plan held the member — Aetna, Humana, or Oklahoma Complete Health — the statewide Children’s Specialty Plan for children in foster care, or fee-for-service: ABD members, SoonerPlan, AI/AN members who kept the exemption, and everything before April 2024. Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishFor thirty years Oklahoma was the great holdout: SoonerCare ran as the state’s own program — a patient-centered medical home model with the state as payer — while nearly every other state capitated. Then, after a ballot initiative, a court fight, and an act of the legislature, SoonerSelect went live on April 1, 2024: the newest Medicaid managed care program in the nation, moving an entire population into three plans essentially at once. That launch is visible in the data — the vertical line where thousands of gray FFS spans end in March 2024 and plan spans begin in April. When a state converts, its history keeps the seam forever, and every converted record carries the same begin date. Anyone who has migrated a large system will recognize the shape.

What the File Says

Oklahoma really does speak X12N 834 — the Oklahoma Health Care Authority’s roster file to its SoonerSelect plans, a file type this state has only been sending since 2024. Pick a member and read the transaction the plan would receive for that member's most recent enrollment event, segment by segment, with a plain-language note on every line.

In plain EnglishAn 834 is the state telling a health plan: this person is yours now (or isn't anymore). Oklahoma’s register D carries the segment worth staring at: an 001 change moving a member from the postpartum or parent category into AE02 — the expansion group installed by State Question 802. Same person, same plan, new group code, coverage unbroken. Hold it against Mississippi’s register D, where the identical life event produces a 024 and then silence. Two files, one 834 standard, one state line — and the difference is a constitutional amendment. Fee-for-service members (ABD, SoonerPlan, tribal opt-outs) generate no plan 834; for them, the rendering below is the state’s own record in the standard shape.
Members are grouped by register; the transaction is rebuilt live from the member's actual spans.
