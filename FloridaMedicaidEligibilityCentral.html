Eligibility Spans

One row per coverage-group span — the state's answer to “from when to when, and under which door did this person enter?” Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishA span is a promise with dates on it — and Florida’s file contains the shortest promises in this series: register C’s Share of Cost months, single-month eligibility spans that switch on the day incurred bills cross the member’s monthly threshold and off with the calendar. Watch the LT01 rows too: 95 of them begin mid-window not with a determination but with a wait-list rank coming up — Florida’s HCBS waiver is a scored queue released in funded batches. And register F holds a negative-space teaching: eligibility spans that do not move at Medicare, because in a non-expansion state there is no expansion category to age out of.

Statewide Medicaid Managed Care

One row per delivery span: an SMMC 3.0 plan — Sunshine, Humana, Simply, Community Care Plan (provider-owned), or Florida Community Care (the LTC specialist, teal) — fee-for-service for the Share of Cost months and dual wraps, or the purple rows: Molina and Aetna, whose spans all end January 2025, when SMMC 3.0 arrived without them. Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishFlorida has run Statewide Medicaid Managed Care since 2014 and re-procures it in cycles — and this window catches a cycle landing: SMMC 3.0, live February 1, 2025. Three things happened at once, and register D separates them cleanly. Plans departed — Molina and Aetna were not awarded contracts, and 150 members here moved, chosen or auto-assigned. The map was redrawn — eleven regions consolidated to nine, so 115 members’ spans run unbroken while the region code beneath them changed meaning overnight: the rename beneath an unbroken span, and every rate cell and report keyed on region needed an effective-dated crosswalk. And plans continued — 110 members whose only trace of February 2025 is a new contract number. One procurement, three data signatures, all inside the window where you can see them.

What the File Says

Florida really does speak X12N 834 — AHCA’s roster files to the SMMC plans, at some of the highest volumes in the country. Pick a member and read the transaction the plan would receive for that member's most recent enrollment event, segment by segment, with a plain-language note on every line.

In plain EnglishAn 834 is the state telling a health plan: this person is yours now (or isn't anymore) — and Florida’s stream teaches with volume and with edge cases. February 2025 was a coordinated burst: termination waves to two departing receivers and addition waves to the survivors, all effective the same day — a re-procurement is, at the wire level, the largest choreographed 834 event a state can produce. Register C’s members generate no plan file at all: coverage that flickers month to month cannot be capitated, so the Share of Cost record below is the state’s own, in standard shape — with begin and end dates inside the same month, the shortest DTP pairs in this series.

All members, identifiers, addresses, phone numbers, and dates are synthetic and deterministic (seeded PRNG, seed 305001); any resemblance to real persons is coincidental.
