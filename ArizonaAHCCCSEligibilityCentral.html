In plain EnglishA span is a promise with dates on it — and Arizona’s eligibility side carries two facts worth pausing on. The ALTCS groups (LTEPD, LTDD) route members into the Arizona Long Term Care System — a second, fully capitated universe running since 1989, with its own contractors and, on the DD side, a state agency serving as the plan. And register C’s split: Arizona’s waiver restricts prior quarter coverage for most adults — their spans begin with the application month — while children and pregnant members keep the classic backward reach, producing the only fee-for-service spans in the entire file.

Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishIn 1982, the last state finally joined Medicaid — on the condition that it could skip everything that came before. AHCCCS launched under an 1115 waiver as mandatory statewide capitation from day one: the oldest continuously running demonstration in the country, and the only program with no fee-for-service past to convert, reconcile, or apologize for. Seven years later Arizona did it again with ALTCS (1989): long-term care — nursing facilities, HCBS, all of it — fully capitated, decades before the states this series has visited worked up the nerve. Two more originals ride the roster: Banner – University Family Care, the academic medical center that owns its plan, and DES/DDD — a division of state government holding a capitated contract like any other plan. And for tribal members, Arizona did not leave the managed care exemption as an absence: it built AIHP, the American Indian Health Program — the choice, formalized, with a card.

What the File Says

Arizona really does speak X12N 834 — AHCCCS has been cutting roster files to plans longer than any Medicaid agency in America; its enrollment EDI predates the HIPAA standards it now uses. Pick a member and read the transaction, segment by segment, with a plain-language note on every line.

In plain EnglishAn 834 is the state telling a health plan: this person is yours now (or isn't anymore) — and Arizona has been saying it since before the standard existed. Watch two things in this file. Register F’s LTSS transitions are plan-to-plan pairs: an ACC termination and an ALTCS addition, same member, same month, with no FFS interlude — the hand-off between two capitated systems is the only kind of hand-off this state has. And register D’s AIHP transactions carry a delivery code that is neither a commercial plan nor an absence: a named program for the tribal FFS pathway, switchable any month in either direction — the most fluid HD segment in this series.

Arizona AHCCCS Eligibility Central — a single-file demonstration app in the Central series. All members, identifiers, addresses, phone numbers, and dates are synthetic and deterministic (seeded PRNG, seed 602001); any resemblance to real persons is coincidental. Program facts reflect public AHCCCS policy of the mid-2020s and change year to year — plan rosters, GSA assignments (simplified to statewide here), and prior-quarter rules shift with waivers and procurements; verify against current AHCCCS and CMS publications before any non-demonstration use.
