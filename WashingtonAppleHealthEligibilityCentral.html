Eligibility Spans

One row per coverage-group span — the state's answer to “from when to when, and under which door did this person enter?” Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishA span is a promise with dates on it — and Washington keeps two kinds of ledger. The HCA side holds the MAGI world: Apple Health for Kids with continuous eligibility from birth to six (register E’s income events that close nothing), the expansion adults, and the state-funded Apple Health Expansion, whose spans carry no federal match and whose cap produced the waitlist rows. The DSHS side holds Classic Medicaid — SSI-related, LTSS, ALTSA assessments. Register F is the bridge between them: the end reason that isn’t a loss of coverage but a change of agency.

Integrated Managed Care

One row per delivery span: which Integrated Managed Care plan held the member — physical and behavioral health in one capitation — the statewide foster plan (Apple Health Core Connections), or Classic fee-for-service. Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishWashington finished, in 2020, what most states are still arguing about: full integration. An Apple Health plan’s capitation covers the primary care visit and the therapy appointment and the substance-use treatment — one plan, one payment, whole person. There is no behavioral health carve to reconcile, no second payment system running under the first. (Colorado’s app in this series is the exact inverse — a capitated behavioral benefit over FFS physical health. Read the two together and you have both ends of the national design spectrum.) The five plans include a genuine original: Community Health Plan of Washington, built and owned by the state’s community health centers — the clinics are the shareholders. Foster children ride one statewide plan, Apple Health Core Connections. And Classic Medicaid — the DSHS side — stays fee-for-service, which is where register F’s members land when Medicare arrives.

What the File Says

Washington really does speak X12N 834 — HCA’s roster files to the Apple Health plans ride through ProviderOne. Pick a member and read the transaction the plan would receive for that member's most recent enrollment event, segment by segment, with a plain-language note on every line.

In plain EnglishAn 834 is the state telling a health plan: this person is yours now (or isn't anymore) — and in Washington, “yours” means all of it: physical, behavioral, whole person, one capitation. Two things to watch in this file. The AHE members’ transactions are indistinguishable from everyone else’s — same segments, same plans — and that is the trap: the funding difference (state-only, no federal match) lives in the coverage group code, not the file structure, and every federal report you build downstream has to remember it. And register F’s terminations are not losses: the member is moving from the HCA ledger to the DSHS ledger — same person, same ProviderOne ID, different half of state government. Read the end reason, not just the 024.
Members are grouped by register; the transaction is rebuilt live from the member's actual spans.
