Las Vegas and Reno have plans; the other fourteen counties are fee-for-service. The City Limit

One row per delivery span: one of the four urban MCOs — Health Plan of Nevada (provider-integrated), Anthem, SilverSummit, Molina — or fee-for-service, which in this state is not a residue but a region: everything outside the Clark and Washoe county lines. Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishNevada draws the sharpest delivery boundary in this series with a road atlas: mandatory managed care inside Clark and Washoe counties, fee-for-service everywhere else. It is not a phase-in or a residue — it is the standing architecture, decided by where the people are (nine in ten Nevadans live in those two counties). The consequence is register D’s teaching: a member who moves from Pahrump to Las Vegas acquires a capitation; one who moves from Henderson to Elko generates a plan termination for a member who lost nothing and chose nothing. The plan ends because the map says so. One more Nevada original rides the procurement: the state’s public option law ties marketplace participation to these Medicaid contracts — the Medicaid procurement as leverage — noted in the glossary, one layer above this file.

What the File Says

Nevada really does speak X12N 834 — DHCFP’s roster files to the four urban MCOs. Pick a member and read the transaction the plan would receive for that member's most recent enrollment event, segment by segment, with a plain-language note on every line.

In plain EnglishAn 834 is the state telling a health plan: this person is yours now (or isn't anymore) — and Nevada’s stream carries two segments worth studying. Register E’s files carry TPL: the other-coverage loop naming the Culinary Health Fund or an employer plan as primary — a segment that changes no dates and no enrollment, only who pays first, which downstream is everything. And register D’s county-line terminations are the strangest 024s in this series: cut for members who lost nothing, chose nothing, and remain fully eligible — the plan relationship ended because an address crossed a line on a map. Rural members generate no plan file at all; theirs is the state’s own record, in standard shape.

Nevada Medicaid Eligibility Central — a single-file demonstration app in the Central series. All members, identifiers, addresses, phone numbers, and dates are synthetic and deterministic (seeded PRNG, seed 702001); any resemblance to real persons is coincidental. Program facts reflect public Nevada Division of Health Care Financing and Policy policy of the mid-2020s and change year to year — in particular, statewide managed care expansion has been on Nevada’s policy calendar, so the urban/rural boundary shown here should be verified against current DHCFP and CMS publications before any non-demonstration use.
