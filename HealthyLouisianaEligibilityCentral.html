Overview

A synthetic Louisiana Medicaid (Healthy Louisiana) population across a 24-month window (January 2025 – December 2026), viewed as of July 2026. Every value is deterministic — seeded PRNG, identical on every load.

Members

The full synthetic roster. Click any row for the member record; click a column header to sort.

In plain EnglishEvery person here has a thirteen-digit Louisiana Medicaid ID that never changes — and note the jurisdiction column: parishes, because Louisiana is the only state that has them. The program’s identity is borrowed data: register C’s express-lane members were enrolled from their SNAP certifications with no Medicaid application anywhere upstream of the record, its ex parte members were renewed without anything being mailed, and register F’s duals had their buy-in accreted from SSA leads data before they reported a thing. Healthy Louisiana reads other programs’ files — it is how the July 2016 launch became the fastest clean expansion in the country, and the machinery never stopped running.

Eligibility Spans

One row per coverage-group span — the state's answer to “from when to when, and under which door did this person enter?” Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishA span is a promise with dates on it — and this file asks a question no earlier app has: what if the determination behind the span belongs to another program? Register C’s express-lane spans opened because a SNAP certification already proved the income test; its ex parte members rolled through renewal with nothing mailed and nothing signed. And register F holds Louisiana’s wall: 325 spans that all begin July 1, 2016 — the launch morning — now ending one birthday at a time. Missouri’s wall of identical dates was set by a court; this one was set by a go-live.

The Six-Plan Market

One row per delivery span: one of six Healthy Louisiana plans — the widest menu in this series, statewide, every parish — or fee-for-service, which in Louisiana is where duals and the waiver LTSS population live. Click a row to open the member.

In plain EnglishSix plans compete for every Louisiana enrollment — and most enrollments are decided by none of the members: register D opens the auto-assignment algorithm, the chooser for the silent majority, whose factor weights (family alignment, prior plan, provider relationships, a quality-weighted lottery) are among the most consequential unread documents in managed care. Behavioral health was folded into these capitations in 2015 — Louisiana ended its behavioral carve-out a year before Washington began ending its own — while duals and waiver LTSS sit outside the plans in fee-for-service. Register E holds the LTSS story worth telling: the registry that replaced the decade queue — Louisiana’s infamous first-come, first-served developmental disabilities waitlist, converted to urgency-based screening: an honest data structure where a dishonest one used to be.

What the File Says

Louisiana really does speak X12N 834 — LDH’s roster files to the six Healthy Louisiana plans. Pick a member and read the transaction the plan would receive for that member's most recent enrollment event, segment by segment, with a plain-language note on every line.

In plain EnglishAn 834 is the state telling a health plan: this person is yours now (or isn't anymore) — and Louisiana’s stream carries additions with a strange property: for the express-lane members, no application exists anywhere upstream of the file. The determination lives in another program’s system; the 834 is the first Medicaid artifact of the member’s life. The ex parte renewals are stranger still — the event the stream barely reports, because the renewal that works perfectly is a non-event by design. And register F’s terminations all close spans that share one begin date, July 1, 2016: when you find a wall of identical dates in enrollment data, the question is always “what happened that day?” — here, the answer is a launch.
Members are grouped by register; the transaction is rebuilt live from the member's actual spans.

Healthy Louisiana Eligibility Central — a single-file demonstration app in the Central series. All members, identifiers, addresses, phone numbers, and dates are synthetic and deterministic (seeded PRNG, seed 504001); any resemblance to real persons is coincidental. Program facts reflect public Louisiana Department of Health policy of the mid-2020s and change year to year — the plan roster, express-lane and ex parte mechanics, and registry chronology are teaching simplifications; verify against current LDH and CMS publications before any non-demonstration use.
